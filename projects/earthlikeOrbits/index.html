EARTHLIKE ORBITS

Modeling Lagrange Points

Designed and Coded by Leah Welch

(with physics consultation and moral support from Steven Pankratz)

In the Sun-Earth Orbital System, the Lagrange Points are the most gently rejecting of other bodies orbiting as fast as the Earth. They are pseudo-stable. Imagine a marble resting on a surface. A Lagrange Point is like a surface that is flat enough that the marble will still roll off of it, but very slowly.

In this simulation, the Earth and the Sun act as static attractors. Random bits of space junk orbit them according to a gravitational constant, adjustable with a slider. There are 5 satellites positioned at the 5 Lagrange points in the Sun-Earth System.

Satellites positioned at a Lagrange Point will be very slowly expelled from that point.

Compare these satellites' velocities to those of the randomly placed space junk orbiting the sun!
